Script Format

Glasspane reads plain .md (Markdown) files. Write what you want to say, add a few annotations for delivery, and drop the file into the app. No special software required ‚Äî any text editor works.

In a hurry? Jump to the Cheat Sheet.

Slide boundaries

Start each new slide with a ## heading. The heading text appears on screen as a section title.

## Opening ‚Äî Inaugural Address

Vice President Johnson, Mr. Speaker, Mr. Chief Justice, fellow citizens:

## Pledges to the World

To those old allies whose cultural and spiritual origins we share,
we pledge the loyalty of faithful friends.

Everything after a ## heading belongs to that slide until the next ## heading. If your script has no headings, Glasspane treats the entire file as one slide.

Spoken lines

Every plain paragraph becomes a line on the teleprompter. Write one thought per paragraph. Glasspane highlights each line as you speak, so shorter paragraphs are easier to follow at a glance.

We observe today not a victory of party but a celebration of freedom.

For I have sworn before you and Almighty God the same solemn oath
our forebears prescribed nearly a century and three-quarters ago.

The world is very different now.

Blank lines between paragraphs are ignored ‚Äî use them freely to keep your file readable.

Inline directions

Place a tag in square brackets anywhere inside a spoken line. The tag appears on screen as a small accent-coloured label. The audience never sees it ‚Äî it is only on your teleprompter.

Vice President Johnson, Mr. Speaker, Mr. Chief Justice, fellow citizens: [BREATHE]

On screen that line looks like this:

Vice President Johnson, Mr. Speaker, Mr. Chief Justice, fellow citizens: BREATHE

Common tags

TagPurpose
[BREATHE]Take a breath before continuing
[PAUSE]Brief pause for effect
[SLOW]Slow your pace for the next phrase
[LOOK UP]Make eye contact with the audience
[SMILE]Smile ‚Äî it changes your vocal tone

Making up your own

Any text in square brackets written in ALL CAPS is treated as a direction. Use whatever works for you:

We exceeded every target. [FIST PUMP]

And we did it together. [GESTURE TO AUDIENCE]

Slow emphasis

Wrap a word or phrase in double asterisks to mark it for slow, deliberate delivery. On the teleprompter the text appears in a distinct colour so you know to slow down.

Let us never negotiate out of fear. **But let us never fear to negotiate.**

On screen that line looks like this:

Let us never negotiate out of fear. But let us never fear to negotiate.

Use slow emphasis sparingly. If everything is emphasised, nothing is.

Block-level cues

A block-level cue sits on its own line ‚Äî it is not part of a spoken sentence. It tells you to do something between lines of speech. There are three kinds.

Click cue

[CLICK]

Signals that you should advance your presentation slide. On the teleprompter it appears as a small highlighted pill:

CLICK

Pause block

[PAUSE ‚Äî let the formalities settle. Survey the crowd.]

A pause with an optional note to yourself. The double dash (--) separates the tag from the note. On screen:

[PAUSE ‚Äî let the formalities settle. Survey the crowd.]

A bare [PAUSE] on its own line works the same way without the note.

Presenter note

[NOTE ‚Äî 20 January 1961. Washington, D.C. Speak slowly ‚Äî your breath will be visible in the cold air.]

A note visible only to you. Use it for reminders that are not part of your speech:

[20 January 1961. Washington, D.C. Speak slowly ‚Äî your breath will be visible in the cold air.]

Inline vs block

The same tag can be inline or block depending on where you place it. If it is the only thing on the line it becomes a standalone block element. If it sits inside a sentence alongside other words it becomes an inline direction attached to that line.

Let us never negotiate out of fear. [PAUSE]    ‚Üê inline: pause cue sits beside the sentence

[PAUSE ‚Äî let the opening land]                 ‚Üê block: pause stands alone between lines

[CLICK]                                        ‚Üê block: advance your slide

fellow citizens: [BREATHE]                     ‚Üê inline: breathe cue sits beside the sentence

Slide dividers

Glasspane automatically inserts a visual divider between slides. You do not need to add anything yourself ‚Äî the ## headings handle it.

For an extra visual break within a slide, insert a horizontal rule:

---

This adds a thin line across the teleprompter at that point.

Annotated example

A short script that uses every feature. Each line has a comment explaining what it does.

## Opening ‚Äî Inaugural Address                  <!-- slide boundary: starts slide 1 -->

[NOTE ‚Äî 20 January 1961. Speak slowly.]         <!-- block-level presenter note -->

[CLICK]                                         <!-- block-level click cue -->

Vice President Johnson, Mr. Speaker,            <!-- spoken line -->
Mr. Chief Justice, fellow citizens: [BREATHE]   <!-- inline direction -->

[PAUSE ‚Äî let the formalities settle.]           <!-- block-level pause with note -->

We observe today not a victory of party         <!-- spoken line -->
but a celebration of freedom.

**We dare not forget today that we are          <!-- spoken line with slow emphasis -->
the heirs of that first revolution.** [BREATHE]

---                                             <!-- visual divider within the slide -->

## A Call to Negotiate                          <!-- slide boundary: starts slide 2 -->

[CLICK]                                         <!-- block-level click cue -->

Let us never negotiate out of fear. [BREATHE]   <!-- spoken line with inline direction -->

**But let us never fear to negotiate.**         <!-- spoken line with slow emphasis -->

[PAUSE ‚Äî 4 seconds. One of the most quoted lines. Let it breathe fully.]
                                                <!-- block-level pause with note -->

**Ask not what your country can do for you** ‚Äî <!-- spoken line with slow emphasis -->

ask what you can do for your country. [LOOK UP] <!-- inline direction -->

Copy this into a .md file, drop it into Glasspane, and you will see every element rendered on the teleprompter.
